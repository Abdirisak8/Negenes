    • Top activities for self-development

    Whenever you ask anyone what they want from life, chances are, they will say they just want to be happy. Happiness, however, is a fluid concept. What makes you happy may not make someone else happy. Moreover, happiness is fleeting; no one can be happy all the time.

    Striving to live “happily-ever-after” is a fool’s errand. Some studies have found that the pursuit of happiness can lead to depression and even suicide. Happiness, after all, is inherently dependent on other people.

    Instead of chasing after happiness, you should concentrate more on making yourself abetter person. Self-development serves two purposes: making you feel good about yourself, no matter what; and positively influencing people around you to do the same. The best part is, you don’t have to go out of your way to make a better life for yourself through self-development. All you need is to develop some good habits. Here are some of the top activities for self-development you can do right now.

    Top activities for instant self-development

  • Make time for yourself
    1. The key word in self-development is “self.” No matter how busy you get, or how famous you become, you should never neglect the importance of “me” time. Schedule at least an hour for yourself that you are not worrying about deadlines, how to do math homework or chores that need doing. Drop everything and curl into your favorite armchair with a good book, watch your favorite television show, chat with your family, or simply do some deep breathing in a dim room. When you take “me” time seriously, you will feel more satisfied with your life.
  • Learn something new everyday
    1. Make a point of doing or learning something new. You could take up a hobby and make new friends. Find out something new about your work, like a new app, or collaborate with others to pick their brains. Something is always out there that you never knew, and finding out not only develops your mind, it makes you a more interesting person.
  • Take a break
    1. The Pomodoro technique is a productivity tool where you work for 25 minutes, and then take a 5-minute break, then work 25 minutes again. After the fourth work period, you take a 15-minute break. Many people find this helpful because it allows them to ease tense muscles and shake the cobwebs from their brains. This is especially important if you spend your entire time sitting. It does not work for everyone, but in most instances, you need to learn to relax to become more productive. This is one of the key habits of successful leaders.
  • Get organized
    1. Most people find it hard to organize their lives, but it is an important skill. An organized life is a purposeful life, and you will find that you get more things done faster and better, leaving you more time to do other things. Start by organizing your workspace, use online tools for education, slowly expanding to all aspects of your life until everything has a place and a place for everything. When you can find what you need when you need it, it saves you time and helps you avoid frustration.
  • Do your best, every time
    1. An important habit to cultivate for self-development is to do your best in anything you do. It does not matter what it is. If you are washing the dishes, do it as well as possible. If you have this attitude for small tasks, it will carry over to everything else you do.
  • Worry no more
    1. It is natural for people to worry, but it is ultimately a useless waste of energy and emotion if there is nothing you can do to promote the desired outcome. Make it a habit to forget about problems and situations that are out of your control, so that you have more energy and time to tackle those about which you can do something. This affects everyone around you in a positive way.

      These are all activities that help you create habits for better and effortless self-development. It takes time to get into the groove, but the journey itself is rewarding. Ultimately, it does not matter what you do; it is how you think that promotes self-awareness and personal improvement.
